States of Matter

Solid Liquid Gas

Solid

Has fixed shape and fixed volume. Particles are closely packed and vibrate in place. Strong attraction between particles. Examples: ice, metal, wood, stone

Liquid

Has fixed volume but no fixed shape. Takes the shape of the container. Particles are close but can slide past each other. Attraction is weaker than solids. Examples: water, oil, alcohol, milk

Gas

Has no fixed shape and no fixed volume. Expands to fill the entire space available. Particles are far apart and move very fast. Very weak attraction between particles. Examples: oxygen, nitrogen, water vapor
